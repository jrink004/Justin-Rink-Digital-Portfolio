Recent Learning Experiences

ENGS 452 Senior Design

Fall 2025 & Spring 2026

Senior Deisgn gave me and my fellow teammates an opportunity to utilize all of our learned engineering knowledge and apply it to a selected problem. During the project, I made use of my CAD, materials, thermal, mechanical, and electrical engineering skillsets to finalize our design.

ENGS 348 Heat Transfer

Spring 2026

Heat Transfer gave me a greater understanding of how energy moves in the physical world. From the course I learned greatly about the transfer of energy through conduction, convection, radiation, and how system design can alter the implications of all of these.

ENGS 355 Structural Analysis

Fall 2025

Structural Analysis gave me a greater understanding of how the physical world operates. From the course, I have a greater knowledge of loading, determinacy, deflection, and modes of failure all experienced in structures.

ENGS 295 CAD II Advanced Design

Fall 2025

CAD II Advanced Design gave me the opportunity to practice and refine my CAD skills through the completion of various projects. The ultimate goal of this course is to pass the CWSA CAD Associate Exam, which I did! Yay!

ENGS 320 Mechanical Dynamics

Spring 2025

Mechanical Dynamics, in my opinion the hardest engineering course at Hope, gave me the opportunity to learn about the mathematics of motion. From the course, I have a superior understanding of kinematics, energy and momentum, vibrations, and planar rigid body motion.

ENGS 346 Fluid Mechanics

Spring 2025

Fluid Mechanics taught me about the behavior of fluids both at rest and in motion. From this course, I mastered the topics of fluid statics, viscous flow and boundary layers, conservation in systems, and internal vs. external flow.
